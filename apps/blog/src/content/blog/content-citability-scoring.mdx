The Princeton GEO study (Aggarwal et al., KDD 2024) tested nine content optimization tactics across 10,000 queries. Five tactics measurably boosted AI citation rates. The data is clear: specific formatting changes directly increase your chances of being cited.

## The Five Proven Tactics

### 1. Statistics Addition: +41% Visibility Improvement

AI engines heavily weight content with verifiable numerical data. When your page contains relevant statistics, AI systems treat it as more authoritative and cite it more frequently.

**How to apply**:
- Include industry-specific statistics in every major section
- Cite the source and year (e.g., "According to Gartner, 73% of enterprises...")
- Use comparison tables with numerical data
- Update statistics quarterly to maintain freshness

### 2. Quotation Addition: +28% Visibility Improvement

Direct quotes from recognized experts or authoritative sources increase citation probability significantly. AI systems use quotations as trust signals.

**How to apply**:
- Include 2-3 expert quotes per 1,000 words
- Attribute quotes with name, title, and source
- Use blockquote markup with cite attributes
- Link to the original source when possible

### 3. Source Citation: +22-26% Visibility Improvement

Inline references to credible sources signal that your content is research-backed. This is especially effective for Perplexity, which prioritizes multi-source validation.

**How to apply**:
- Link to authoritative domains (.edu, .gov, major publications)
- Reference industry studies with author and year
- Include a "Sources" or "References" section
- Use descriptive anchor text, not "click here"

### 4. Authoritative Tone: +21-23% Visibility Improvement

Content written with confident, expert tone is cited more often than tentative or promotional language. AI systems detect linguistic authority signals.

**How to apply**:
- Use active voice and definitive statements
- Avoid hedging language ("might," "maybe," "possibly")
- State conclusions clearly
- Support every claim with evidence

### 5. Fluency Optimization: +21% Visibility Improvement

Well-structured, readable content is easier for AI systems to parse and synthesize. Poorly organized content is skipped even if the information is valuable.

**How to apply**:
- Use answer-first paragraph structure
- Maintain logical heading hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)
- Keep paragraphs to 3-4 sentences
- Use lists and tables for complex information

## What Does NOT Work

The Princeton study also identified tactics that either had no effect or performed negatively:

- **Keyword stuffing**: Performed negatively across all platforms
- **Vague link text**: "Click here" and "read more" reduce citation probability
- **Excessive jargon**: Without plain-language summaries, citation drops
- **Generic claims**: Unsubstantiated superlatives are ignored

## Combined Strategy: 30-40% Improvement

The study found that combining multiple tactics produces compounding effects. The optimal combination is:

1. Add relevant statistics to every major claim
2. Include expert quotations for key assertions
3. Cite authoritative sources inline
4. Write with confident, evidence-backed tone
5. Structure content for easy AI extraction

Pages implementing all five tactics saw 30-40% improvement in AI citation rates across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude.
